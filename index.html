Staying on Track

Practice with a metronome. Always. Start slower than you think you need to. Speed comes from accuracy, not the other way around.
Record yourself regularly. Even a quick phone recording once a month. You won't notice daily progress, but monthly recordings will shock you.
Play with other people. Bluegrass is community music — jam sessions accelerate learning faster than anything. You're in the Shenandoah Valley — prime bluegrass territory. Look for local jams and festivals.
Don't abandon your strengths. You already play well by ear with great chord knowledge. This plan adds a new layer on top of a strong foundation.
One concept at a time. If a week's topic doesn't click, stay on it. Rushing creates shaky foundations. The goal is understanding, not speed.
